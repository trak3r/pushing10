# Pushing10

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Broad casual audience — anyone who enjoys short, satisfying logistics puzzles. Play sessions last seconds to minutes: board passengers, choose a route, watch the flight land. The game is single-player, no auth, no persistence decisions; pick up and put down at any time.

## Product Purpose

A minimalist air-transport logistics game. You own a fleet of planes at various airports. Passengers appear at your current airport with destinations and coin rewards. Board the ones whose destinations align with profitable routes, manage fuel costs, and grow your coins. Success is building a mental model of the route network and making increasingly efficient choices.

## Positioning

Minimalist logistics puzzle — strip the tycoon genre down to its most satisfying loop: match passengers to destinations, manage range and fuel, watch the flights land.

## Brand Personality

Retro, chunky, fun. The interface has a deliberate arcade-terminal feel: uppercase titling, bold gold accents, chunky cards, and a dark cockpit-like background. It should feel satisfying and snackable, not dense or sim-like.

## Anti-references

Too complex / sim-like. Avoid dense data tables, spreadsheet-like layouts, overwhelming numbers, or multi-tab management UIs. The game's depth comes from route planning, not from interface complexity.

## Design Principles

- **Satisfying, not overwhelming.** Every screen shows only what the player needs for the next decision. Hide complexity behind progressive disclosure.
- **Chunky and tactile.** Buttons, cards, and badges should feel substantial. Generous padding, bold borders, deliberate use of color to signal state.
- **The loop is the product.** The board → fly → land → profit cycle should be visible at a glance. The UI is a cockpit, not a spreadsheet.
- **Retro warmth.** Dark backgrounds, glowing accent colors, uppercase display type. The terminal aesthetic without the sterility.
- **Mobile-first.** Designed for one-handed thumb use on a phone; the 480px max-width layout is a constraint to embrace, not fight.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Reasonable defaults: 4.5:1 body text contrast, visible focus indicators, support for reduced motion via `prefers-reduced-motion`. No formal WCAG target for v1.
